Strategy

The whole thing in a paragraph.

Your keyword universe splits cleanly across your two products. Product 1 (join a virtual job fair — city/type events you host) is the job-seeker traffic and candidate-supply engine; it's working. Product 2 (host a hiring event — your Indeed-style self-serve product) is where the money is, and it's almost entirely uncaptured.

~15,000
employer buyer-intent searches a quarter see JobFairX in Google results — and almost none click. You rank #1 for "virtual hiring event platform" (~10,000 impressions/qtr) with ~0 clicks, because Google shows your job-seeker homepage ("attend a free job fair / get hired"). Employers shopping for a platform skip it. Meanwhile you barely rank (page 2–3) for the "host a hiring event" terms that describe the same product.

The fix is one idea: make /employer the strong, employer-worded hub that owns the platform + host cluster, capture the employers already landing on your job-seeker pages, and measure all of it. Don't build new products on keyword volume alone.
