1. One-time asset uploads

Upload each PNG once. Store the returned image_id in config. Reuse across every event forever.

Upload sequence (per asset)

  1. POST /media/upload/?type=image-event-logo → returns S3 pre-signed URL + upload token
  2. PUT image bytes to S3 URL → HTTP 204
  3. POST /media/upload/ confirm with token → returns image_id

3. Per-event API sequence

Run these 4 calls in order for every new event. Fully automated.
1Create the event shell

POST /v3/organizations/{ORG_ID}/events/

{
  "event": {
    "name": { "html": "{City} Healthcare Virtual Job Fair – {EventDate}" },
    "start": { "timezone": "{TimezoneId}", "utc": "{StartDateTime}" },
    "end":   { "timezone": "{TimezoneId}", "utc": "{EndDateTime}" },
    "currency": "USD",
    "online_event": true,
    "listed": true,
    "shareable": true,
    "logo_id": "COVER_IMAGE_ID"
  }
}

Returns the new event_id.

2Push the Overview body

POST /v3/events/{event_id}/structured_content/?purpose=listing

Send the modules array. See section 4 for the full payload structure.

3Add FAQ items

For each of the 14 FAQ items: POST /v3/events/{event_id}/questions/

Use Eventbrite's dedicated FAQ component, not the description body. The FAQ component adds schema.org markup which gives an 8% organic traffic boost.

4Publish

POST /v3/events/{event_id}/publish/

Event goes live on Eventbrite.

5. Sanitizer rules — text module

Per Eventbrite's structured_content docs, the text module accepts plain text or HTML. Only the tags below are allowed.

Allowed tags (official)

  • <p> — paragraph
  • <h1>, <h2>, <h3> — headers
  • <b>, <em> — bold & emphasis
  • <a> — link
  • <ul>, <ol>, <li> — lists
  • <aside> — caption
  • <blockquote> — quote

Stripped before render

  • Inline style attributes (no backgrounds, borders, padding, widths, colors, fonts)
  • Class names, IDs
  • <div>, <span> containers (styled or not)
  • <button> — buttons cannot be rendered in text modules
  • <table>, <tr>, <td>, <br>
  • SVG, embedded <img> (images go in their own module)
Implications for our template:
  • The 3-step graphic, the logo grid, the trust stats banner — uploaded as image modules.
  • The employer CTA "Reserve your spot →" is wrapped in <h3><a> for prominence — no button styling will survive.
  • Any rules, backgrounds, or borders shown in the mockup are for design preview only — Eventbrite output is flat text + Eventbrite's default tag styles.

6. Native Eventbrite config (outside the description body)

These settings sit on the event object itself, not in structured_content.

Event metadata

  • Refund policy: "Free event — no refund required"
  • Tags: virtual, online, job fair, careers, {city}, healthcare
  • Language: en_US
  • Capacity: large or uncapped (recommend 10,000+)
  • Online event: true

Series

Group all healthcare events under one Eventbrite series. SEO compounds across cities when events are sibling-linked.

Highlights chips

Eventbrite auto-renders these from event metadata. Should show: 4 hours, Online, English.

Ticket class

Single free ticket class:

{
  "name": "Free admission",
  "free": true,
  "quantity_total": 10000
}

7. Cover image specs

  • Recommended size: 2160×1080 (2:1 aspect)
  • Min size: 940×470
  • Format: PNG or JPG
  • Content baked in: title "Healthcare Virtual Job Fair" + gold "Free for Job Seekers" pill
  • No city name, no date — universal across all 1,000+ events

8. Updating assets later

When we refresh a graphic (e.g., new tile design, updated logo set):

  1. Upload the new PNG → get new image_id
  2. Swap the image_id in config
  3. Future events use the new asset automatically
  4. To update existing live events, run a patch script: POST /events/{id}/structured_content/ with the new payload

Frequently asked questions

FAQ lives in Eventbrite's dedicated FAQ component — NOT in the description body. Each Q/A pair is a separate Eventbrite FAQ item with schema.org markup (the 8% organic traffic boost).
How does JobFairX work for job seekers?
After you register on Eventbrite, we send you a message to complete your registration on JobFairX. You create an account, upload an existing resume or build a free one through JobFairX, and we match you with employers actively hiring for roles that fit your background. You request interviews with the employers you want to meet, and if accepted, you'll have a scheduled video interview on event day.
How do you match job seekers with employers?
Our matching system reviews your resume, skills, experience, location, and job preferences against open roles from employers attending the event. Roles that align with your profile are surfaced for you to request an interview.
How can I prepare for the virtual job fair?
Complete your JobFairX profile with up-to-date skills, experience, and job preferences. Upload a current resume or use JobFairX's free resume builder. Research the employers and roles you're interviewing with. Test your computer, internet connection, microphone, and camera ahead of time. Dress as you would for an in-person interview.
Is there a fee to participate?
No. Registering, getting matched, requesting interviews, and attending interviews on JobFairX is 100% free for job seekers.
What should I do before my scheduled interview?
Make sure you have a stable internet connection and a quiet, well-lit environment. Test your microphone and webcam. Review the employer and job requirements. Have your resume ready to share.
Can I conduct the interview from a mobile device?
Yes, you can use a smartphone or tablet. A laptop or desktop is recommended for the best experience, but mobile works if your connection is stable.
What if I need to reschedule my interview?
Log into your JobFairX account and go to your scheduled interview. You can reschedule directly from your dashboard. Let the employer know if possible.
When will I hear back from the employer after my interview?
Timing varies by employer. Some give immediate feedback, others take days or weeks. If the employer mentioned a timeline during the interview, wait until that period passes before following up.
How do I participate in a JobFairX virtual job fair?
Register on Eventbrite, then complete your registration on JobFairX. Once your profile is complete, our matching system surfaces open roles for you. You request interviews with employers you want to meet. If accepted, you'll receive a scheduled interview time and join the video interview on event day.
What time does the event run?
All JobFairX events run from 11:00 AM to 3:00 PM local time. Your specific interview time is assigned individually based on the employer's schedule and your accepted requests. Registration closes when the event starts.
Can I attend from anywhere?
Yes. JobFairX events are accessible from anywhere with an internet connection.
How do I join my scheduled interview?
Log into your JobFairX account on event day. Your scheduled interview details and join link will be in your account.
How does the interview itself work?
Interviews happen by video through JobFairX. Log in about 5 minutes before your scheduled time. Most interviews last 15–20 minutes. Test your microphone and camera in advance. Dress professionally. Thank the interviewer at the end.
What if I have technical difficulties during the interview?
Check your internet, refresh your browser, verify your microphone and camera are working. Try a different browser or device if needed. Contact JobFairX support if the issue persists.
