This Isn't a Job Board. It's an Interview Pipeline.

Job boards give you applicants. We give you a full day of scheduled pre-screened interviews.

  • Candidates pre-matched to your roles
    Your open roles are matched against registered candidates in your city. Only qualified candidates are invited to request an interview.
  • Every interview is earned
    To get on your schedule, candidates submit their resume and answer your screening questions. No one gets through without qualifying.
  • Your schedule is full before the event begins
    On event day, your interview pipeline is ready with 20 to 100+ pre-qualified interviews, scheduled and waiting in a single four-hour virtual event.

FAQ

The questions employers actually ask.

Indeed and LinkedIn give you applications — resumes in a pile that you then have to sort, screen, email, and schedule. JobFairX gives you scheduled interviews on a specific date. Candidates are pre-qualified through your screening questions and book themselves into your calendar before the event. You don't post and wait. You register and show up.
Show rates on JobFairX events run high because of the structure: candidates pick their own time, receive automated reminders, and get a confirmation message when you accept. For the few who can't make it, the platform automatically captures them in a follow-up list so you can schedule a 1:1 conversation after the event.
Yes. Growth plans cover up to 3 jobs per event. Pro covers up to 6. Each role can have its own screening questions and required qualifications, so the candidates who show up are pre-matched to the right job.
Most employers go from registration to live in under an hour. Pick an event, add your jobs (use AI-generated descriptions or paste your own), set screening questions, and invite interviewers. Candidates start matching within 24 hours.
No. Live video runs directly inside JobFairX — no Zoom, no Teams, no Google Meet. The candidate's resume, your interview notes, and in-call chat sit alongside the video so you never switch tabs.
