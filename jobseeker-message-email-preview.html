What changed and why

  • Light background. Renders reliably across every email client. Dark backgrounds break in Gmail's light mode and Outlook.
  • Sender identity block. Nike's brand pill + recruiter's name adds legitimacy. In production, swap the "N" pill for the real company logo.
  • Personalized greeting. "Hi Alex" — feels like a real message, not a system notification.
  • Two-line header hierarchy. Big: "Nike sent you a message." Small: job + city + recruiter name.
  • Reply on JobFairX button. Deep-linked back into the message thread — you keep the conversation, the data, and the response-rate metrics on your platform.
  • Timestamp. "Sent 2 hours ago" up top gives context.
  • Legal footer. Unsubscribe + preferences + "why am I getting this" — required by CAN-SPAM. Current email is technically non-compliant.
  • Subject line + preview text. "Nike wants to interview you for Retail Sales Associate" is outcome-focused. Way better open rate than a generic "New message" line.
