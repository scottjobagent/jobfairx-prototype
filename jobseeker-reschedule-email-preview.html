What changed and why

  • Reschedule framing instead of invitation. The subject and headline now say Nike proposed new times — not invited you to interview. The candidate already knew they had an interview.
  • "Was" reference. Shows the original time crossed out so they immediately understand what's changing.
  • Amber reschedule callout. Signals "attention needed but not urgent" — distinct from a first invite (green/blue) or a cancellation (red).
  • Reassurance line. "Your spot is still held" and "This is just a time change, not a cancellation" — addresses the anxiety the candidate feels when they see a reschedule email.
  • Deduped time list with day of week. Current email showed "Sept 23 2023, 11:30 AM PDT" twice + one date with no time. Day-of-week + calendar tile makes the times scannable.
  • "Choose a new time" CTA. Accurate to what they're doing. "Schedule Interview" implies they had nothing before — they did.
  • Escape hatch: "Suggest another time." If none of the proposed times work, they still have a path forward — instead of ghosting the employer.
  • Legal footer. Unsubscribe + preferences links, CAN-SPAM compliant.
